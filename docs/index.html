Missouri COVID data

The data on this page was updated Wednesday, December 15, 2021. For the values that are averages over 7 days, the start date is Monday, December 6, 2021 and the end date is Sunday, December 12, 2021. The original data comes from the Missouri Department of Health and Senior Services.

Vaccinations

An average of 22,681 vaccine doses have been administered per day for the previous 7 days.

New cases

There have been an average of 2,531 new cases per day for the previous 7 days. This number includes infections confirmed by PCR tests as well as probable cases indicated by antigen tests.

There have been an average of 1,904 new confirmed cases per day for the previous 7 days. These cases are the result of positive PCR tests, which are are more sensitive to the virus and therefore more accurate. However, not everyone receives a PCR test.

There have been an average of 627 new suspected cases per day for the previous 7 days. These are the result of positive antigen tests, which are not as sensitive as PCR tests. If someone has a positive antigen test and later receives a positive PCR test, they are removed from the "suspected" count and added to the "confirmed" count.

The positivity rate of PCR tests over the last 7 days is 12.4%, with an average of 16,680 PCR tests administered per day. The positivity rate of antigen tests over the last 7 days is 7.5% with an average of 9,580 antigen tests administered per day.

Outcomes

There are currently 1,887 people hospitalized with COVID-19.

450 people are in intensive care.

251 people are on ventilators.

Over the last 7 days, an average of 4 people have died from COVID-19 each day. Note that death reporting can be delayed, so tracking this number over a longer duration will help to understand what is happening in the state.
